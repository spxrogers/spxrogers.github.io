builder → showcase

Four UX directions for turning the builder role into a showcase of past projects. Each opens as a full, clickable page that feels like the live site.

Option 1
Project cards

A responsive grid of cards — name, blurb, tech tags, link-out. Visual and scannable; the most "portfolio" feeling.

Best when: projects have logos/links and you want them to pop.
Option 2
Timeline

A chronological vertical trail. Leans into the "more ideas than time" narrative and shows momentum over the years.

Best when: the story / progression matters more than visuals.
Option 3
Expandable list

Compact list of names + years; click to reveal detail. Keeps the card tidy even with lots of projects.

Best when: you have many projects and want a low-scroll default.
Option 4
Featured + sub-tabs

A "gist / projects" toggle keeps the current blurb, plus one highlighted "now building" feature over a tidy list of the rest.

Best when: you want to keep the intro AND spotlight one thing.
Note: projects (Cellar, PaceMaker, Toast…) are placeholders to show the layout — swap in your real ones. All four reuse the live site's design tokens (Inter / Fraunces, the #3a5a8c accent, card styling) so the chosen direction can drop straight into _includes/roles/builder.html. Content could later move to a _data/projects.yml file so adding a project is a few lines of YAML.
